Bruce Lee

The father of Jeet Kune Do
Bruce (left), in a scene from 'Way of the Dragon' with Chuck Norris. This fight scene within the Colosseum in Rome, is one his most well known.

Bruce Lee, born Lee Jun-Fan on November 27 1940 in San Francisco, was a renowned martial artist and actor and was one of the biggest pop culture icons of the 20th century.

Bruce spent his early years in Hong Kong and appeared in 20 films by the age of 18. Despite his acting, Lee was continually getting into trouble due to fighting. One of the biggest changes in his life was when he began to study with Yip Man at the age of 16. Lee flourished in the Wing Chun style, but ultimately had to leave Hong Kong due to his street fighting.

Lee arrived in America at 18. He completed his high school education and enrolled in college. It is here he started teaching others martial arts. In 1964, Lee demonstrated his 'one-inch punch' at the Long Beach International Karate Championships. Not long after this, the infamous fight with Wong Jack Man took place. This fight is the reason that Jeet Kune Do was born, as Lee has said that the fight took too long and the techniques were too rigid.

Lee had some small acting roles in the US, most notably as Kato in The Green Hornet, but ultimately left the US for Hong Kong after he felt his idea for a TV show, Kung Fu, had been stolen. Lee arrived in Hong Kong in 1971, already famous for his Kato role. He signed a two picture deal and the first of those, The Big Boss, was released that year. It was a huge success for Lee. Lee would make 4 other movies before his death on July 20 1973.

    Here's a time line of major events in Bruce Lee's life:

  • 1940 - Born in San Francisco, California
  • 1946 - Has his first film role in 'The Beginning of a Boy' in Hong Kong
  • 1953 - begins training in Kung Fu with Sifu Yip Man
  • 1959 - returns to the United States
  • 1963 - meets his future wife Linda Emery and opens the Jun Fan Kung Fu Institute
  • 1964 - marries Linda and attends the Long Beach International Karate Championships where he demonstrates the 1 inch punch and 2 finger pushup
  • 1965 - Brandon Lee born, Bruce's first child. Bruce's father dies and he attends funeral in Hong Kong. Wong Jack Man incident
  • 1966 - Bruce stars in 'The Green Hornet' as Kato
  • 1969 - Shannon Lee born
  • 1971 - injures his sacral nerve and begins writing 'The Tao of Jeet Kune Do'. Moves to Hong Kong and films the movie 'The Big Boss' in Thailand. Loses part for 'Kung Fu' to David Carradine
  • 1972 - films 'Fists of Fury' in Hong Kong and travels to Rome to film 'The Way of the Dragon' which he also writes and directs. Brother James dies
  • 1973 - films 'Enter the Dragon'. Bruce has several occasions where he collapses and blacks out and eventually dies of a cerebral edema on July 20. His funeral in Hong Kong is attended by 25,000 people and he is eventually buried in Seattle, Washington, USA
